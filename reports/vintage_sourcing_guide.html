💡 Sourcing Tips

  • BHF Furniture Stores - Dedicated furniture stores that can take large items
  • Oxfam - Often has dedicated book + music shops; slightly higher prices but quality stock
  • Emmaus - Community-based, known for furniture and homeware
  • Check high streets with multiple charity shops - e.g., "Caterham High St has 6 charity shops"
  • Oxfam receives Zara donations - Unsold stock donated to select shops

🎪 Jumble Sales 56 mentions

Key Finding: Jumble sales are community-based events, typically at church halls, village halls, and schools. Extremely cheap prices but requires local knowledge to find.

Types of Jumble Sales

  • Church Jumble Sales - Most common type
  • Village Hall Sales - Rural community events
  • School Fetes - Often have jumble/secondhand sections
  • Scout/Guide Jumble Sales - Fundraising events
  • NCT Nearly New Sales - Baby/children items specialist
  • Jumble Trail - Multi-location neighborhood events

💡 Finding Jumble Sales

Check local Facebook groups, church notice boards, and community newsletters. Often advertised only locally with short notice.

"I've been to boot sales, flea markets, antique fairs, small village Christmas events, even school fetes"
